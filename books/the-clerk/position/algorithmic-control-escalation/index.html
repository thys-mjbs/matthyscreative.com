Operational Risk Memo: Automated Compliance Engines

System Architecture

The engine ingests requests, applies rule sets, and issues approval tokens. Tokens gate payments and settlements. Logs feed audit dashboards. Overrides are permitted with supervisor credentials and tracked.

Governance and Metrics

Primary metrics include variance reduction, throughput time, and exception rate. Departments are ranked. Penalties apply to high override counts. Policy changes require modeled improvements to metrics.

Operational Risks

Model rigidity during cost shocks leads to denial cascades. Override deterrents suppress corrective action. Vendor pools shrink as suppliers conform to approval patterns.

Escalation Paths

As denials rise, management issues rule patches that restrict categories. Payment gating enforces compliance. Parallel procurement channels emerge, reducing transparency.

Exposure and End State

Authority consolidates in system outputs. Human review capacity atrophies. Reported compliance remains high while adaptive capacity declines.
