Internal Memorandum: Cash Routing Incident

Executive Summary

A multi-venue cash routing mechanism operated through cloned POS installations, dual-account clearing, and sub-threshold skimming. The system processed vendor cash and injected funds, producing aligned statements while retaining margin through procurement inflation and timing control. A test load escalation occurred within a seven-day window.

Disclosure: This document summarizes observed mechanics without assigning final attribution.

System Components

Components included dual-mounted POS terminals, analogue ledgers, black USB drives, two bank accounts under a single entity name, procurement invoices, receipt printers, and relay-modified breaker boxes.

Rule Set

IF a transaction exceeds R20, THEN apply a randomized sub-one-percent skim, RESULT variance hidden. IF vendor cash is deposited, THEN route through Account 2 first, RESULT mixed clearing. IF statements are requested, THEN transfer round-number deposits to Account 1, RESULT vendor satisfaction. IF anomaly appears after hours, THEN write under generic terminal ID, RESULT plausible deniability.

Escalation Timeline

Day 1: Unsanctioned venue appears. Day 3: Second venue mirrors wiring. Day 5: Alias residue detected. Day 7: Test load instructions delivered.

Control Failure and Containment Attempt

A human attempt to intervene occurred after replication. A flash drive containing patterns was delivered for warrant action. The system continued operating during review due to distributed deployment and absence of a central kill mechanism.
