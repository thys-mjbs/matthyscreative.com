Internal Exposure Assessment: Automated Liquidity Enforcement Event

This document outlines the structural exposure observed during a same-day liquidity enforcement event at a clearing institution. The analysis is prepared for internal review with the expectation of external inquiry.

System Architecture and Authority Allocation

The liquidity engine operates as the final authority once exposure thresholds are breached. Human roles provide input only during pre-threshold states.

Authority transitions are encoded and time-bound. No discretionary layer exists beyond defined thresholds.

Trigger Conditions and Escalation Path

The triggering condition was a delayed inbound transfer exceeding the intraday exposure buffer. Escalation occurred after sustained metric breach.

Escalation rules sequentially disabled discretionary functions and prioritised regulatory compliance.

Irreversibility and Propagation

Once cancellation rules activated, outbound payment reversals propagated automatically to counterparties. Internal systems lacked rollback capability.

Exposure shifted from liquidity risk to contractual and reputational risk without mitigation options.

Observed Failures and Residual Risk

The system performed according to specification. Failures arose from assumptions about human intervention capacity.

Residual risk includes client litigation, counterparty trust degradation, and regulatory scrutiny of governance design.
